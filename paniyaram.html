Kuzhi Paniyaram

Paniyaram, also known as Kuzhi Paniyaram or Gunta Ponganalu, are savory, spiced, crisp, and soft dumplings made from leftover idli batter. They are easy to make and can be made gluten-free by skipping asafoetida in the recipe.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Idli Batter
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped Onions (or 1 medium-sized onion)
  • 2 tablespoons grated Carrot (optional)
  • 2 chopped Green Chilies (skip for kids)
  • 1 teaspoon grated Ginger
  • 6 torn Curry Leaves
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped Coriander Leaves (optional)
  • Â½ teaspoon Mustard Seeds
  • Oil (for seasoning and frying)
  • Salt (if needed)

Instructions

  1. Mix all the ingredients (except oil) in a bowl.
  2. Heat a paniyaram pan and add oil to each cavity.
  3. Pour the batter into the cavities and cook until golden brown.
  4. Serve hot with chutney or sambar.

Tips

Batter Consistency: Ensure that the batter is well-fermented. Fermentation imparts a tangy flavor and lightness to the paniyaram. The batter should be thick but pourable. Adjust the consistency by adding water if needed.

lavorful Additions: Customize your paniyaram by adding ingredients like finely chopped onions, green chilies, grated ginger, curry leaves, and coriander leaves. Optionally, temper the batter with mustard seeds, cumin, hing (asafoetida), and grated coconut for authentic South Indian flavors.
